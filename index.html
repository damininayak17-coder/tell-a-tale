Our Story

Hospitality as a
form of belonging.

I never really had one home growing up. Home was wherever I was — a hostel bunk, a relative's spare room, a new city that didn't know my name yet. That taught me something early: the place and the person hosting you matter more than any amenity. They can make you feel like you belong, or like a stranger.

Tell A Tale started from that feeling. A belief that hospitality, done with intention, can make someone feel genuinely at home — even somewhere entirely new. Every guest I welcome, every space I curate, any stories we chase or any event we bring to life that turns strangers into friends — it's all rooted in that same quiet hope: that no one should feel out of place in a place they're expecting to rest.

This is small, intentional, and very much still becoming. But the feeling I want every guest to leave with? That one's already figured out.

"You will never be completely at home again, because part of your heart always will be elsewhere. That is the price you pay for the richness of loving and knowing people in more than one place." We hope to keep a piece of yours with us.

On The Horizon
For Property Owners

Your property,
in good hands.

Tell A Tale partners with homeowners who want their space to be cared for thoughtfully — without the usual stress of managing tenants, maintenance coordination, guest communication, or day-to-day logistics from a distance.

We handle the experience end-to-end: guest screening, check-ins, upkeep coordination, communication, and ensuring your home is treated with warmth, responsibility, and attention to detail.

For some owners, this means transforming an underused space into something alive again. For others, it simply means peace of mind — knowing their property is being looked after by people who genuinely care how a space feels and how it is experienced.

More than just short stays, we're trying to build spaces people remember fondly long after they leave.
